My love for the Napoleonic Wars started in my teens. Weird right? Not something a young lad would typically get into. It started when I was slightly bored on holiday in Scotland. We had no signal and my step dad, who was reading another Sharpe book, offered me Sharpe's Tiger which is based on England’s war with France in India. I loved it so much I soon began reading the other Sharpe books in the series.

I then started reading non fiction books about the war interspersed with Sharpe. My interest and fondness for this period has since exploded. It has taken me to Paris and Edinburgh to look specifically for Napoleonic artifacts.

Hanging proudly on my office wall is an oil painting of the Battle of Trafalgar… next to a Pacha Ibiza poster. I am also the owner of a signature from Rowland Hill, 1st Viscount Hill.

My wife and I flew to Paris for the day and visited the Musée de l'Armée. I wanted to see the damaged cavalryman's breastplate from the Battle of Waterloo, and Napoleon's Tomb.

Most recently I made the trip to Edinburgh specifically to see the Closing the Gates at Hougoumont painting by Robert Gibb. I also saw the captured French Imperial eagle by Charles Ewart during the Battle of Waterloo. Quickly followed by a couple of pints in the Ensign Ewart pub just down from Edinburgh Castle.

One of my all time favourite anecdotes focuses on the 92nd Highlanders. During heavy exchanges with the French, Wellington sent the Scots Greys cavalry forward.

“They were all Gordons, and as we passed through them they shouted, 'Go at them, the Greys! Scotland for ever!' My blood thrilled at this, and I clutched my saber tighter. Many of the Highlanders grasped our stirrups, and in the fiercest excitement dashed with us into the fight.”
